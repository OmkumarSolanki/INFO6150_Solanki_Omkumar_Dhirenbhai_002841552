Ice hockey is a fast-paced team sport played on an ice rink, where two teams of skaters, typically consisting of six players each, compete to score goals by shooting a puck into the opponent's net. Players use curved sticks to handle the puck and aim to outmaneuver their opponents while skating on ice. The game is known for its physicality, with body checking and collisions being integral parts of the sport. Ice hockey is immensely popular in countries like Canada, the United States, Russia, and several northern European nations, and it has a dedicated following at both professional and amateur levels. Its thrilling combination of speed, skill, and strategy makes it a captivating and enduring winter sport.
